Neural networks can express more than they learn, creating expressivity-trainability gaps. Our paper, â€œMind The Gap,â€ shows neural networks best learn parallel algorithms, and analyzes gaps in faithfulness and effectiveness.

We characterize expressivity-trainability gaps via neural compilation, which compiles a source program into neural network parameters that implement it perfectly. Our paper is the first to introduce neural compilation for GNNs.

Compiled solutions act as constructive expressivity proofs for a given architecture. In particular, we analyze the disparity between learning breadth-first search (BFS) and depth-first search (DFS), and then analyze Bellman-Ford.

Even when controlling for trace length or trace complexity, we find there is still a large disparity between BFS and DFS, indicating the issue is not the training data, but the underlying algorithm:
