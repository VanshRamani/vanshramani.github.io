  Travel & Experiences

Singapore üá∏üá¨ (ICLR 2025)
ICLR 2025 in Singapore was an incredible experience! Gardens by the Bay provided the perfect backdrop for evening strolls where conversations about neural architecture search continued under the towering Supertrees. The marriage of technology and nature felt symbolic of the conference itself.

Sentosa Island offered a welcome respite from intense technical discussions. Beach walks with Samyak Jain turned into impromptu whiteboard sessions in the sand, sketching graph structures and discussing the future of AI research.

Late-night walks through Singapore's immaculate streets with Mridul Gupta became a cherished ritual. We'd recap the day's highlights and often lose track of time discussing everything from transformer architectures to the philosophical implications of AGI. (Yes, I lost my debit card during one of these adventures, but the memories far outweighed the inconvenience!)

  Academic Life & Philosophy

Current Mood: Quizzes and Academics üòÖ
Right now, I'm just trying to work my way through quizzes and academics. :3 The life of an undergraduate researcher involves a delicate balance between coursework, research deadlines, and conference submissions. Sometimes I wonder how I manage to juggle graph neural networks, molecular chemistry, and linear algebra problem sets all at once!

Research Philosophy
I believe that the best research happens at the intersection of disciplines. My journey from molecular solubility prediction to graph distillation to high-dimensional search algorithms reflects this philosophy. Each field informs the others - the mathematical rigor from chemistry, the algorithmic thinking from computer science, and the statistical foundations from machine learning.

Learning Mindset
Every conversation with researchers like Prof. Michael Bronstein, Prof. Pradeep Ravikumar, and Dr. Sayan Ranu teaches me something new. I try to approach every problem with curiosity and humility - there's always more to learn!
